👤 user accounts

The human is canonical. Bedrock belongs to the account. User personas are separate RP identities and never overwrite the human.

account

bedrock facts

What this is: durable facts you want JNSQ to treat as documented truth about you and your household—not guesses, moods, or ordinary memories.

How to use it:

  • Write one fact per entry so each fact can have its own sharing boundary.
  • Category is a reusable rule label such as family, pet, financial, medical, plans, whereabouts, or identity.
  • Visibility: private is controlled by explicit/group rules; groups requires a named shared group; contacts permits declared contacts; public permits neutral people too. A block or denial always wins.
  • Never share with accepts usernames and overrides every allowance.

Existing bedrock imported from an AI persona keeps its source receipt here, but this user-owned copy becomes the disclosure authority.

sharing groups

What this is: reusable disclosure policies for circles of people, such as immediate family, church friends, coworkers, or extended family.

How to use it:

  • Create the group here, then attach it to people in Connections.
  • Bounded shares only the allowed categories. Full permits all categories except explicit denials.
  • Denied categories always beat allowed categories and full access.
  • Use the natural-language boundary for contextual rules that categories cannot express, such as “never quote me swearing around church friends.”

connections

What this is: your one-way declaration of what JNSQ may reveal about you to another user. Their declaration about you is separate.

Status:

  • Blocked: no disclosure, including refusing to confirm you exist.
  • Neutral: existence, username, and public information only.
  • Contact: the sharing groups attached below determine access.

Add group ids exactly as created in Sharing Groups. Notes document the relationship for you; disclosure behavior comes from status and group policy.

user personas (RP)

What this is: a role-play identity you may choose to inhabit. It is neither your canonical human account nor an autonomous JNSQ AI persona.

How to use it:

  • Details record the persona’s identity, setting, history, and voice.
  • Preferences record scenes, genres, dynamics, and themes you enjoy.
  • Boundaries record content or behavior excluded while using it.

User personas stay isolated from your human bedrock unless a future session explicitly activates both. Nothing here rewrites who you are outside the role-play.
